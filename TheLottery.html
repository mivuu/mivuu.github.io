The Lottery

information architecture

Where is your money really going? A financial tragedy.

What I did

ABOUT

This interactive informational design piece serves to raise awareness on the harsh realities of the lottery system, displaying the process from the moment the customer buys the false advertising, to the ending point of revealing the higher classes' excessive greed. It all ties down with the system's strong selling point being hope.

    BREAKING IT DOWN

    Participant's behaviours

    Surprisingly, majority of lottery buyers still purchase a ticket even though they acknowledge the chances is slim.

    Demographics

    Lottery buyers have a dominated male to female ratio. Though females have been found to be buying lottery tickets when the draw is near.

    What are your chances?

    There are many different combinatations when it comes to certain lottery games. But how does one go about really expressing the impossible feeling? I wanted to compare the odds of winning the lottery to other seemingly impossible events to make it feel comparable to users.

    How a dollar travels

    Users are able to fully understand how the lottery usually breaks down and who it goes to by simply breading it down to a dollar bill.

    REFLECTION

    Challenges

    This project challenged my way of thinking when I was given the job of making both a print and interactive version of this informational project.

    I found the print piece being the most difficult due to it's size and shape. My goal was to somehow find a way to be convincing, engaging, and helpful yet without going overboard with information and data.

    Learning Outcomes

    Some things I have learned from this experience is that the narrative is really important on how we gain attention from users/participants. Without it, information data becomes less interesting for many.
