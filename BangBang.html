Bang! Bang!

Product design, UI/UX

Designing an experience that overcomes the barriers and ideals of what it is like to play tennis.

What we did

THE ISSUE

Tennis has always had barriers when it comes to participating or even starting it, examples of these barriers are the sport is too expensive, lack of partners, and being more serious than it is fun and many more.

Our approach

SOMETHING
UNIQUE

Together with two other group members, Stefana and Tony, we concepted a tennis vibration damper, called Bang! Bang! This smart object is a fun and interactive device that overcomes the barriers and ideals of what it is like to play tennis.

What does it do?

Bang! Bang! emits sounds, selected by the user through the app, randomly every time the user hits the tennis ball with the racquet thanks to the sensor implemented into the smart object.

Our findings

THE RESEARCH

Target Audience

We aimed our product to those who haven’t played tennis yet or were intimidated by it.

With research, we discovered that most are daunted when watching pro tennis players, such as Serena Williams, and therefore making people feel intimidated.

Stakeholders

This product can also be beneficial for:

  • Tennis Clubs: Add some spice to the usual routine if users want to change up the game every now and then by allowing players to rent the smart device.

  • Instructors: Provide another way method to allow students to enjoy the sport rather than have students play competitively against each other.

    • Product Goals

    The smart device that we conceptualized needed to be fun and enjoyable so that users are able to create a overall better experience. In addition, this product would help players to consistently keep playing the game by encouraging the continuation of the sounds being played.

    For this product to work, we had to make sure that the users would actively engage in the app like how they would with our smart object.

    We decided that the app, even though simple and intuitive, needed to be more inviting when it comes to it’s overall interface, making it so that the mood of the app will contribute to the game play.

    Smart Device

    BRINGING IT
    TO LIFE

    We wanted to create a working prototype that would provide a better understanding of how the system is developed for users. It also offered quick user feedbacks that lead to better solutions for the smart device.

    Low-fidelity

    THE START

    Breaking up the app into three simple sections; Home, Store and Statistic. We decided that the Apple Watch app was a great way to start off the foundation of our compainion app where everything was accesible within an instant of connection the smart device.

    High-fidelity

    A VISUAL
    WALKTHROUGH

    Connecting

    When the app opens, users are right away asked to connect their smart device to the application by capturing a photo of the QR code on the smart device.

    Home

    Once connected, users will have access to everything they would need to start using their smart device on the Home page. From controling the volume to making a playlist, users can right away start playing.

    Statistic

    Users are able to fun statistics that the smart device has recorded, such as:

  • Productivity - How often the users play
  • Total hits
  • Longest rally

    • Users can also compare these statistics with friends and family as well!

    Store

    The store is based off of the Apple's App Store to give a familiarity usage when navigating through the store.

    Users have a variety of themes that they will have easy access to when on the main page of the Store:

  • Trending
  • Sale
  • Suggestions

    • The store is also based off of themes throughout the year. For example, users will see sounds based of what holiday or event that is going on in the duration of when the user is browsing the store.
